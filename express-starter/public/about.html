About Husky Hero

The student-built guide to navigating UW courses — built by Huskies, for Huskies.

🎯

Our Mission

Choosing the right courses shouldn’t feel like a gamble. Husky Hero gives every UW student access to honest, peer-written reviews and open community discussions so you can make informed decisions — before you ever click “Register.”

We believe that the best advice comes from the students who just sat in those seats. No algorithm, no sponsored listings — just real experiences from real Huskies.

⚡

How It Works

1

Browse Courses

Search and filter UW courses by department, course number, or keyword. Get a quick snapshot of difficulty, workload, and overall rating at a glance.

2

Read & Discuss

Dive into student reviews for honest, detailed takes on coursework, instructors, and expectations. Join threaded discussions to ask follow-up questions or share your own experience.

3

Register with Confidence

Walk into registration knowing exactly what you’re signing up for. Build a schedule that fits your goals, your pace, and your life.

✨

What We Offer

📝

Peer Reviews

Star ratings for overall experience, difficulty, and workload — plus free-form written takes from students who’ve been there.

💬

Discussion Threads

Reddit-style nested threads on every course page. Ask questions, share tips, or warn the next generation of Huskies.

📊

Course Stats

Aggregated averages for difficulty and workload give you a data-driven view alongside the stories behind the numbers.

🔍

Smart Search & Filter

Filter by department or search by name and number to find exactly what you’re looking for in seconds.

💜

Our Values

  • Student-first. Every decision we make is guided by what helps students, not what benefits institutions or advertisers.
  • Honest over flattering. We don’t filter out criticism. A tough-but-fair review is worth more than a dozen glowing ones.
  • Community-driven. Husky Hero only works when Huskies contribute. Every review you write makes it better for everyone.
  • Open & accessible. No paywalls, no accounts required to read. The knowledge belongs to all Huskies.
